Telco Monthly Charges: Prediction & Pricing Engine

Machine Learning · Regression Analysis · Interactive GUI
Dataset Telco Customer Churn (Kaggle) Records 7,043 Customers Target MonthlyCharges ($) Models Linear Regression · KNN · Decision Tree Year 2024
This project develops a regression-based pricing prediction engine for a telecommunications provider, predicting a customer's monthly charge from 19 behavioral and service features. Three machine learning models — Linear Regression, K-Nearest Neighbors, and Decision Tree Regressor — were trained, evaluated, and deployed through an interactive Tkinter GUI, enabling real-time price estimation per customer profile.
Introduction

Pricing in telecommunications is driven by a complex mix of customer demographics, contracted services, and usage patterns. Manual pricing estimation is both error-prone and inconsistent.

This system applies supervised machine learning to learn pricing patterns from historical customer data, producing a reliable predictor of monthly charges for new and existing customers.

A graphical user interface allows non-technical stakeholders to generate predictions by selecting customer attributes from dropdowns without writing any code.

Key Insights
Fiber Optic customers pay ~60% more per month than those with no internet service.
Month-to-month contract holders have the highest average charge, suggesting premium flexibility pricing.
KNN achieves the best R²=0.91 by leveraging similarity of customer profiles — neighbors with the same services pay similar prices.
TotalCharges and tenure are the strongest numeric predictors of monthly spend.
Churn flag is retained as an input — churned customers show different pricing patterns, improving model accuracy.
Business Impact
The pricing engine enables customer-facing teams to instantly estimate appropriate monthly rates, supporting consistent pricing across sales channels.
